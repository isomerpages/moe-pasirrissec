<h3>Frequent Asked Questions</h3>
<h4>Streaming Procedures</h4>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Can a student change his/her option after he/she has selected the option?</strong>
</p>
<p>Students can log in to Allears to change his/her options before the deadline
on <strong>27 October 2026 at 5.00p.m</strong>. The latest option saved
in the database would be used for the streaming exercise.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>If my child / ward is not allocated the combination/ elective of his/her choice, can we appeal?</strong>
</p>
<p>Yes. The appeal can be conducted online from <strong>2– 3 Nov 2026, 6.00pm</strong> once
the streaming results are released.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Who can I contact should I need further clarifications?</strong>
</p>
<p>You can contact the HOD of Science Mr Richmond Ang, or contact us at 6501
0800.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Under what circumstances will my child/ward not get the subject combination of his/her choice?</strong>
</p>
<p>Other than not meeting the pre-requisite criteria for subject/s within
the subject combination of his/her choice, your child/ward may not get
the subject combination of his/her choice due to the following reasons:
<br>
<br>i. Insufficient student interest in a subject to start a class (ie. <strong>minimum class size of 15 students)</strong>
<br>
<br>ii. Lack of resources to fully meet the demands of the subject (for example,
lack of teachers)
<br>
<br>iii. The maximum enrolment capacity is met for elective subjects using
special facilities i.e. Art, Design &amp; Technology (D&amp;T).</p>
</li>

<p><strong>Is Additional Mathematics a compulsory subject for admission into JC or polytechnic?</strong>
</p>
<p>No, polytechnic admission only requires English language (or another L1
e.g. Higher Mother Tongue), 2 relevant subjects and 2 other best subjects.
For engineering courses, the relevant subjects must include the Mathematics
and Science subjects, depending on the choice of study. The Mathematics
subject could be either Elementary Mathematics or Additional Mathematics,
whichever has a better grade. The same criteria apply for JC. Additional
Mathematics is recommended if the student wants to pursue Science Stream
in JC. For the latest admission requirements, do refer to the respective
tertiary institution websites.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>What should I consider when choosing between combined Science and Pure Science subjects?</strong>
</p>
<p>Students are encouraged to opt for pure sciences if:
<br>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>they would like to pursue science or science-related courses in the polytechnics
or junior colleges,</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>they are considering Engineering or science-related careers,</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>they enjoy the depth and breadth of pure science subjects.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>
<br>Students who are:
<br>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>able to see relationship e.g., interpret data and graphs</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>focused, precise and detailed,</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>able to visualise and make connections to real-life applications.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
</li>
<li>
<p></p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Will I be able to meet the requirements of polytechnic courses if I do not read O-Level pure sciences?</strong>
</p>
<p>Students are required to take at least one science subject. Pure Sciences
are not prerequisites to pursue polytechnic courses including Engineering
/ Life Science courses.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Does the order of the subject combination matter?</strong>
</p>
<p>Yes, it matters. Students are advised to order their options according
to their strengths and preference.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>I am interested in doing Medicine in NUS / NTU. Do I need to take Biology and the other 2 pure sciences in secondary school?</strong>
</p>
<p>To do Medicine in NUS (Yong Loo Lin School of Medicine) / NTU (Lee Kong
Chian School of Medicine), a student is required to have a good H2 pass
in Chemistry and a good H2 pass in either Biology or Physics at A-Level.
This means that students need to do well in Chemistry and either Biology
or Physics at O-Level in order to continue to pursue these subjects at
A-Level. Please refer to the university websites for more information on
the subject prerequisites.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>If my child is not doing well in Secondary 3, can he/she change subjects in Secondary 4?</strong>
</p>
<p>The school has in place an ECG curriculum that seeks to help your child
choose the right subject combinations. However, if students are not coping
well with a particular subject at the end of Sec 3, appropriate ECG advice
will be given by the school to help students make the necessary changes
to pursue what they want at the post-secondary education institutes (PSEI).</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Can my child take additional GCE level subject as a private candidate?</strong>
</p>
<p>In any given year, a candidate can only sit for GCE level examinations
as either a school or private candidate. As such, it is not possible for
your child/ward to offer additional subjects as a private candidate.</p>
</li>
</ul>
